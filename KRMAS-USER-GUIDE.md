# KRMAS Instructor App — User Guide

*A practical guide for everyone who uses the app: junior instructors, instructors, school
administrators, and superadmins.*

This guide is organised by what you want to **do**. Tasks that only certain roles can do are
tagged, e.g. **(Admins)**. If you don't see a button mentioned here, it's almost always
because your role doesn't have access to that feature — that's normal.

**Your role determines what you can do:**

| Role | In short |
|---|---|
| Junior instructor | See your classes, edit lesson plans, file incidents, volunteer to cover |
| Instructor | The above, plus flag classes for cover and see students & incidents |
| School admin | Everything for **your** school: people, timetable, rostering, notices, events, grading, reports |
| Superadmin | Everything, across **all** schools |

---

## Part 1 — Getting started

### Install the app on your phone or computer
KRMAS is a web app you can install so it behaves like a normal app with its own icon.

- **iPhone/iPad (Safari):** open the app's web address, tap the **Share** button, then
  **Add to Home Screen**.
- **Android (Chrome):** open the address, tap the **⋮** menu, then **Install app** / **Add
  to Home screen**.
- **Computer (Chrome/Edge):** open the address and click the **Install** icon in the address
  bar.

Installing also lets the app work when you briefly lose signal.

### Sign in
1. Open the app. You'll see a sign‑in screen asking for your **email** and **password**.
2. Enter them and press **Enter** or tap **Sign in**. (Use the **Show/Hide** toggle if you
   want to check your password as you type.)

**First time?** Your administrator creates your account and gives you a **temporary
password**. Sign in with it, and change it to something memorable (see "Change your
password" below).

If sign‑in doesn't work, the most common reasons are a typo in the email/password, or an
account that hasn't been confirmed yet — ask your administrator to check.

### Set a device PIN (optional)
You can add a quick **PIN lock** so the app asks for a short code when you open it on your
device. Find **Device PIN** in your account area, choose a code, and confirm it. This is a
convenience lock on your device — it's separate from your sign‑in password.

### Turn on notifications (optional but recommended)
To get alerts on your phone — for example when a class you're the backup for needs cover:

1. Go to the **Me** tab.
2. Tap **🔔 Enable notifications** and allow the permission prompt.
3. You can tap **Send test notification** to confirm it's working.

You do this once per device. If you use the app on your phone and your laptop, enable it on
each.

## Part 3 — Everyday tasks (everyone)

### See your classes and the timetable
Tap **Roster**. You'll see the days of the week along the top and the classes for the
selected day, each showing its time, type, and who's rostered (lead, assist, junior, and
backup). Classes you're personally rostered on are highlighted.

### See your own schedule and hours
Tap **Me**. This shows the classes **you** are teaching, the ones you're listed as **backup**
for, and your hours. (If it says "Instructor not found," your login isn't linked to a roster
person yet — ask your admin to add an email to your roster entry.)

### Read announcements and notices
Tap **Feed** for posts and announcements. Important notices also appear as **banners** at the
top of your screen. If a post is marked **required reading**, open it and acknowledge it so
your admin knows you've seen it.

### Lesson plans
Tap **Plans** (or open a class on the Roster and use its lesson‑plan button). You can read
the plan for a class, and if your role allows, create or edit it. Each class shows its topic
for that week automatically.

### Events calendar
Tap **Events** to see upcoming events. (Adding events is an admin task — see Part 6.)

## Part 4 — Cover for classes

KRMAS routes cover requests to the person listed as the class **backup**.

### Request cover for a class **(Instructors)**
1. On the **Roster**, open the class you can't make.
2. Tap **Need cover**.

The class is marked as needing cover, it appears on the **Cover** tab for everyone, and the
person listed as the **backup** is notified — both with an in‑app banner and (if they've
enabled notifications) a push on their phone.

### You've been asked to cover (you're the backup)
If a class you're the backup for needs cover, you'll see a red **"Cover needed — you're the
backup"** banner at the top of the app, with a **Take it** button. Tap it to pick up the
class. If you enabled notifications, you'll also get an alert on your device even when the
app is closed.

### Volunteer to cover any open class **(Junior instructors and up)**
1. Tap the **Cover** tab to see all classes needing cover.
2. Open one and tap **Volunteer to cover** (or **Take it**).

You'll be assigned as the lead for that class and it'll show as confirmed.

> For cover requests and volunteering to save correctly, the people involved need a login
> linked to their roster entry. Admins: make sure staff are added **with an email**.

## Part 5 — For instructors

### View students
Tap **Students** to see the student roster for your school, with belt/rank and details.
(Adding, editing, and deleting students is an admin task.)

### File and view incident reports
1. Tap **Incidents**.
2. To file one, use the **+ / New incident** option and fill in the details.

Anyone instructor‑level and up can view incidents and file new ones; editing or deleting
existing reports is limited to admins. Filed incidents are stamped with your name
automatically.

## Part 6 — For school administrators

Everything in this part is reached from the **Admin** tab (⚙), which only admins see. As a
school admin, these tools act on **your own school**. (A superadmin sees all schools — see
Part 7.)

### Manage people and their logins
This is the single place to add staff and control who can sign in. A person on the roster
**and** their login are one and the same.

**Add a person:**
1. Admin → **User management**.
2. Tap **+ Add person**.
3. Enter their name, **email**, and **role**.
4. Save. If you gave them an email, they're added to the roster **and** get a login, and
   you'll see a **temporary password** to pass on to them (privately — it's shown once).

A person added with no email is **roster‑only** and can't sign in. You'll see **"Can sign
in"** next to people who have a login, and **"No login"** for those who don't (add an email
and re‑save to give them one).

**Change someone's access:** change their **role** in the same screen — it updates their
sign‑in access immediately.

**Remove someone:** open them and **Delete** — this removes them from the roster **and**
revokes their login.

**See all sign‑in accounts:** the **All sign‑in accounts…** link shows every login,
including any that aren't roster people (such as the owner account).

### Import students in bulk
Admin → **Import users** lets you upload a CSV to add many students at once. They're added
to your school.

### Edit your timetable
1. Admin → **Schools / locations**. As a school admin you'll see **your school** only.
2. Tap **Timetable** to add, edit, or remove class slots (day, start/end time, type, label).
3. Use **Edit** to change your school's details (contact, active days).

You can only edit your own school's timetable and details. (Setting up brand‑new schools is a
superadmin task.)

### Roster staff onto classes
There are two levels:

- **Default staff per slot:** Admin → **Class assignments**. Set who normally leads/assists
  each timetable slot.
- **A specific day's class:** on the **Roster**, open a class and tap **Edit roster** to set
  the lead, assist, junior, and backup for that particular date, mark it cancelled, or add a
  note.

### Post notices
Admin → **Notices board**. Create banner notices for your school — handy for "no class this
Friday" or urgent messages. Notices appear at the top of staff screens.

### Manage events
Admin → use **Event types** to define categories and **Import events** to bulk‑add calendar
events. Events show up on the **Events** tab for staff.

### Grading
Tap **Grading** (or via Admin) to set up and manage grading sessions and record results.
Managing grading is admin‑level; instructors can view.

### Compliance
Admin → **Compliance** tracks instructor requirements and their status, so you can see who's
current and who needs attention.

### Groups
Admin → **Groups** lets you organise staff/students into groups for the school.

### Onboarding
Admin → **Onboarding** tracks new‑instructor onboarding checklists and progress.

### Reports and dashboard
Admin → **Dashboard** for an at‑a‑glance overview and **Reports** for detail. Use **Export
roster** to export the week's roster.

## Part 7 — For superadmins (owner)

A superadmin can do everything an admin can, but across **every** school, plus a few
owner‑only tasks.

### Switch between schools
You can change which school you're viewing/managing. Your changes apply to whichever school
is currently selected, so check you're on the right one before editing.

### Add or manage schools
Admin → **Schools / locations** shows **all** schools, each with **Edit** and **Timetable**.
Use **+ Add new school** to create a new location (this button is only available to you).

### Network‑wide notices and events
Where a notice or event has no specific school, it's organisation‑wide — only a superadmin
can create those. They appear for everyone.

### People across schools
In **User management** you can administer logins in any school, and you're the only role that
can grant the **superadmin** role.

## Part 8 — Your account & getting help

### Change your password
Use your account/sign‑in settings to change your password — do this the first time you sign
in with a temporary one, and any time you want a fresh one.

### Change your device PIN
Find **Device PIN** in your account area to set or change the quick unlock code on your
device.

### Sign out
Use **Sign out** in your account area. You'll be returned to the sign‑in screen.

### Common questions

**I didn't get a notification.**
Make sure you tapped **🔔 Enable notifications** on that specific device and allowed the
permission. Notifications are per‑device.

**The "Me" tab says "Instructor not found."**
Your login isn't linked to a roster person. Ask your admin to open your entry in **User
management** and make sure it has your email.

**I made a change and it disappeared after I reopened the app.**
This usually means your role doesn't have permission to save that particular change, so it
couldn't sync. Check with your admin that you should be able to do it.

**I can see a button but tapping it does nothing.**
The action is likely limited to a higher role (for example, only admins can edit the
timetable). If you believe you should have access, ask your admin to check your role.

**A new staff member can sign in but shows "No login."**
Their account exists but isn't fully linked yet — an admin should re‑save their entry (with
an email). If it persists, the administrator may need to update the back‑end (a deployment
step).

**I forgot my password.**
Ask your administrator to reset your access; they can issue a new temporary password.

---

### A note on what each role sees
If this guide mentions something you can't find, it's by design: the app shows you only what
your role needs. Juniors and instructors get a focused set of tools; admins get the full
**Admin** menu for their school; superadmins get everything across all schools. When in
doubt, your school administrator (or the owner) can confirm what your account can do.

*End of guide.*
